A couple of days ago something happened that left me absolutely mortified. It
was rooted in a miscommunication and it's prompted me to be a lot more
rigorous, possibly pedantically so, but I think it'll be best for everyone.

{{ toc() }}

## Dog sitting

For the past 6 years or so I've had a side gig of [being a professional dog
sitter]. No, this wasn't a COVID-19 thing — I had in fact started doing it
less professionally before that, and had decided around the end of 2019 that
it needed to be more professionally managed. Like for everyone else, COVID-19
was pretty disruptive to this[^1].

[being a professional dog sitter]: https://dogsitter.services/

There isn't much money in dog sitting so it remains more towards the hobby end
of the spectrum, but I do find it a big help with my mental health.

It's very different to computer touching, especially my brand of computer
touching which doesn't have much of an interface with the customers. They can
send email and support tickets, but when they do the thing we're talking about
will still just be computers. Dogs on the other hand, for most pet owners, are
members of the family. And I'm in their homes while they're not there.

It's not like I think of it as like a doctor, nurse, carer or childminder, but
it's still a caring profession. I hope my computer touching customers would
not be upset to read that in many ways I think the stakes can be higher.

I've made mistakes with the computer touching before of course. There's been
some miscommunications. But on the whole, people don't have a deeply emotional
attachment to their computer things[^2].

So what happened?

## "See you in April!"

I received a message from an existing dog sitting client, someone with two
greyhounds that I'd looked after a few times in 2025 and once so far in 2026.
They were just sending me a photo of their dogs and then ended with,

> > See you in April!

But I didn't have any bookings for them in April. Only July and August.

This sort of thing has happened a few times before and it's always been people
forgetting their own plans.

> I don't have anything for you in April. I've got July and August

I responded. I was a bit on edge now. They didn't get back to me for quite a
while, and then it came.

> > I thought we'd agreed dates in April, May, June, and July as well as
> > August??

> Oh no

## The misunderstanding

I _do_ have a web site with a way for clients to see the bookings and request
new ones but like I say: caring profession, personal connection — most clients
want to arrange things over chat message or face to face. This clearly
presents some challenges and I'm normally pretty good about being clear with
people: it's not a booking until I say it's reserved.

I frantically looked back through our chat history and found this back in
November of 2025:

> > Hi Andy, i have a few more dates and wanted to check if you are free, have
> > looked at your diary so hope these work: April … - … / May … - … / may … -
> > … June / June … - … and sept … - … - thanks v much!!!

> Hello, I'm ill in bed with the flu at the moment but briefly yes all of
> these are fine I would rather not the September dates but can do if really
> required. Just let me know!

Then:

> > Hi Andy, hope you have got over the flu. Re the sept dates no worries as
> > will rope my parents in, but thought i shld check summer as you are bound
> > to bet booked up. Wld you free on … july to … july and … august to …
> > august. Might they work at all? Thanks v much

> Yes either/both of those would be fine

> > Great, both dates pls. Thanks v much

> Great! I will mark these as reserved now 😀

So firstly, when someone fires four separate dates at me I consider that to be
them checking my availability against their possible plans and I expect them
to come back to me and say what they _actually_ want. That's what I thought
their second part was (July and August), and so that's all I reserved for
them.

Secondly, when I said I was ill in bed with flu it was because I had a really
bad fever, couldn't sleep, was having hallucinations and experiencing "altered
reality". It actually turned out to be a kidney infection and I had to have an
emergency GP appointment two days after this to get some antibiotics. I'd been
a bit out of it.

It turns out that they really did want to book _six_ trips all at once, and
had assumed I had done that for them. I had in fact only reserved two of them.
And now months have passed and I have taken other bookings.

## Mortification 💀

While I did think my reading of the conversation had been reasonable, that did
not stop me being utterly mortified. Nothing like this had happened in the 6
years I've been doing this professionally.

Checking my calendar, I could still accommodate one of their trips. Three of
them overlapped with other bookings by either one day or two days. Even that
was amazingly lucky — to not have other bookings smack in the middle of what
they'd wanted.

I gave them the bad news, while replaying all of this constantly in my head,
cringing so hard at every angle of it that my Adam's apple and rectum were
continually meeting at my centre of gravity and then collapsing again while
dreading their response.

I was honestly gearing myself up for them being really angry at me, and either
them telling me they'd engage another sitter, or else me having to tell them
that the relationship had broken down and that I couldn't keep them as a
client.

Thankfully, it did not go like that.

> > Pls dont feel awful - i shld have been clearer. I will see what we can do
> > re moving flights around and come back to you tmw

## Regrouping

I still feel a bit nauseous that they had to change three flights because of
this, but that's what they did, and it's all agreed now. I do take some
professional pride that they really want me to be the one who looks after
their dogs that they clearly love a great deal. With seven sittings on the
books for 2026 they're now my biggest clients by total number of days.

Aside from my feelings of cringe, this isn't going to be without negatives for
me. There's a limit to how much I want to be away from home dog sitting, and I
was already near that with the bookings I already had for 2026.

I've felt obligated to take on the four extra ones because of this
miscommunication so I'm going to be away more than I would otherwise have
chosen. Some bookings now run right up against others, so I'll be going home
some evenings, spending a night in my own bed and then going straight to
another booking the next morning. Not normally something I agree to.

I'm thankful too that my partner is understanding about this.

I cannot let this kind of thing happen again.

## Conclusion

I'm just going to have to be more pedantic about making people aware that it's
not a booking until I say it is.

I could go the route of forcing clients to use a web interface, and for a bug
business that would be the right thing, but these clients appreciate the
personal touch and I don't want to force them to do that. I'm just going to
have to be more rigorous.

While on the one hand I shouldn't be so hard on myself about the
miscommunication, and should take heart that my clients do value me, I think
the cringe factor will be helpful in keeping me rigorous with this. I don't
think I'll ever be able to forget how bad this made me feel, so I'll never
leave another booking conversation unclear again.

I do realise how lucky I am that

- they happened to message me and say, "see you in April!"
- the overlaps with existing bookings weren't worse
- they were understanding and able/willing to modify their flights

It could have been so much worse!
